## 🛰️ About us

**OBA Space** is an aerospace startup based in Málaga, Spain, founded in 2024 by engineer and technology entrepreneur **Carlos Martín**. We currently have a multidisciplinary team of over 10 specialists working on space hardware R&D.

Our core technology focuses on the design and deployment of reusable infrastructures to optimize the Delta-V (Δv) required in orbital transfer maneuvers and the active capture of space debris.

## ⚙️ Technological Development Lines

We are developing two parallel system architectures focused on orbital sustainability and efficient transport:

### 1. Electromagnetic Launch Nodes (EML)
An in-orbit electromagnetic accelerator network architecture. The system is designed to inject payloads from LEO (Low Earth Orbit) architectures into higher transfer orbits.
* **Supported Payloads:** Standardized configurations (CubeSats), scientific instrumentation, and life support/spare parts modules.
* **Operational Impact:** A 100% reusable system, designed to minimize the total propellant mass required per mission for extra-LEO transfers and dramatically increase the viable launch window.

### 2. Active Debris Removal Module (ADR)
Development of a dedicated collector vehicle (pod) for environmental remediation missions in low orbits.
* **Operational Impact:** Designed under the paradigm of low operational cost reusability, seeking to make a high ratio of iterative capture of non-cooperative orbital fragments economically viable (Kessler Syndrome mitigation).

## 📊 Tech Stack & Engineering Domains

Our team tackles complex challenges across multiple disciplines:
- **Electromagnetic Systems:** Design of mass drivers and superconducting magnetic topologies.
- **GNC (Guidance, Navigation, and Control):** Attitude estimation coupled to impulsive nonlinear dynamics.
- **Thermofluids:** Management of the thermal gradient generated during electromagnetic sequences and the capture pod's stress.
- **Flight Software:** Fault-tolerant architecture, real-time telemetry processing, and vision systems for non-cooperative rendezvous (debris).
